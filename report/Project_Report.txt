**INTRODUCTION**
This project was completed as part of the Digital Literacy course to understand the importance of digital skills in today’s world. As a Student Digital Ambassador, the goal was to create awareness among peers about safe and effective use of digital platforms. 
The project includes tasks like creating an infographic, building a digital profile, exploring online platforms, learning email etiquette, and understanding cybercrime. Each task helped me gain practical knowledge and improve my confidence in using digital tools.
Overall, this project helped me understand how to stay safe online, communicate professionally, and build a strong digital presence, which will be useful in both academics and future career.

**TASK-1**
For this task, I created a digital literacy awareness infographic using Canva. The infographic includes important topics such as safe internet practices, useful digital tools, and email etiquette. I tried to keep the design simple and visually appealing so that anyone can easily understand it.
While making the infographic, I used icons, colors, and short points to present the information clearly. One interesting part of this task was learning how to explain concepts visually instead of writing long paragraphs.
Initially, it was a bit challenging to fit all the information into one page without making it look crowded. But after adjusting the layout and spacing, I was able to create a clean and readable design. This task improved my creativity and presentation skills.

**TASK-2**
In this task, I created profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and HackerRank to build my digital portfolio. GitHub is useful for showcasing projects and coding work, while LinkedIn helps in building a professional network. HackerRank is helpful for practicing coding and improving problem-solving skills.
I updated my GitHub profile by adding a README file with my basic details and goals. On LinkedIn, I filled in my education details and created a simple professional profile.
In the coming years, I plan to regularly update my GitHub with projects, stay active on LinkedIn, and practice coding on HackerRank. This task helped me understand how important it is to maintain a professional online presence from the beginning.

**TASK-3**
For this task, I used HackerRank and Google Forms. On HackerRank, I solved a beginner-level problem called “Solve Me First,” which helped me understand basic coding concepts.
I also created a Google Form with five questions related to digital literacy. The form included both multiple-choice and short-answer questions. I checked the responses in Google Sheets, which helped me understand how data is collected and analyzed.
This task was useful because it introduced me to platforms that I can use regularly. HackerRank will help me improve my coding skills, and Google Forms can be used for surveys and academic work.

**TASK-4**
Poor digital communication can create confusion and leave a bad impression. For example, if a student sends an email without a subject line or proper greeting, the message may look unprofessional and may be ignored.
In such cases, it is important to follow proper email etiquette, such as writing a clear subject, using polite language, and keeping the message structured. This task helped me understand how to communicate professionally through emails and avoid common mistakes.

**TASK-5**
While working on cybercrime awareness, I realized how common phishing scams have become. It was surprising to see how easily people can be tricked by fake messages and websites that look real.
After learning about this, I have decided to be more careful while clicking links or sharing personal information online. I will always verify sources before trusting them. This task helped me understand the importance of staying alert and following safe online practices.
